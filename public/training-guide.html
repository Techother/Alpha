Overview

System Overview & Architecture

MKL Health is a cloud-based Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM) platform designed for cardiovascular practices managing chronic-care patients. It automates CMS billing compliance, collects daily vitals via SMS, and surfaces clinical alerts before they become crises.

01Core Technology Stack

ComponentTechnologyPurpose
Frontend AppReact 19 + Vite · TypeScriptClinical dashboard, patient management, billing
Authentication & DatabaseSupabase (PostgreSQL)Secure login, patient records, check-ins, alerts
Patient CommunicationsTwilio SMSDaily check-in messages sent to patient phones
Team NotificationsSlackReal-time clinical alerts pushed to care team channels
AppointmentsGoogle CalendarPatient scheduling, follow-up appointments
Project TrackingAirtableBacklog stories, sprint management
Hosting & APIVercel (serverless)All API proxy functions, HIPAA-ready infrastructure

02User Roles & Access Levels

RoleAccessPrimary Responsibilities
Patient SMS interface only — no dashboard login Respond to daily check-ins, track medications, receive SMS reminders
Clinician Full dashboard (read + write on own patients) Monitor vitals, respond to alerts, run screenings, manage care programs, log RPM time
Admin Full dashboard + Setup panel + all configuration User provisioning, integration keys, billing oversight, Slack routing, system health

03Signing In & Authentication

MKL Health uses Supabase authentication with email + password. All API calls carry a short-lived JWT verified server-side — credentials never reach the browser bundle.

1 Navigate to the application URL

Open https://cardiotrack-three.vercel.app in Chrome, Safari, or Firefox. The app redirects unauthenticated users to the sign-in screen.

2 Enter credentials

  • Enter your work email address
  • Enter the password provided by your Admin (change it on first login)
  • Click Sign In

3 Session management

  • Sessions last until you explicitly sign out
  • Click your initials / avatar in the top-right → Sign Out
  • Do not share credentials — each clinician must have a unique login
⚠ HIPAA Requirement: Always sign out when leaving an unattended workstation.

Section A
Patient Workflows

Patients interact with MKL Health entirely through SMS — no app download required. All phone-based workflows are described here. Clinician-side enrollment steps are noted where relevant.

A1Patient Enrollment & Onboarding

Enrollment is performed by a clinician in the Patients section of the dashboard. The patient receives an SMS confirmation once enrolled.

1 Clinician creates the patient record

  • Navigate to Patients → click Add Patient
  • Enter: full name, date of birth, cell phone number, primary diagnosis (ICD-10), assigned provider
  • Enable RPM, CCM, or TCM enrollment as appropriate

2 Obtain written consent

  • Print or email the RPM consent form from the Setup panel
  • Patient (or guardian) must sign before first SMS is sent
  • Upload signed consent document to the patient's chart
⚠ CMS requires documented patient consent before billing CPT 99454.

3 Send welcome SMS

  • In the patient record → click Send Test SMS to verify the phone number
  • The patient receives: "MKL Health: This is a test message from your care team. Reply STOP to unsubscribe."
  • If the patient replies STOP, flag the chart and use an alternate contact method

4 Patient orientation call (recommended)

  • Call the patient to explain: daily check-ins start tomorrow at 9 AM
  • Explain the response format: they reply with numbers or short answers
  • Confirm they can receive and reply to texts on their phone

A2Daily SMS Check-In Flow

Every enrolled patient receives an automated SMS check-in each morning. The platform collects responses and creates a check-in record in Supabase.

1 Patient receives morning SMS

Example message: "Good morning, Maria 👋 Time for your daily MKL Health check-in. How are you feeling today? Reply 1 (great) through 5 (very unwell)."

2 Patient responds to prompts

  • Reply with a number 1–5 for wellness score
  • Follow-up prompt asks for today's weight in lbs
  • Optional: additional prompts for blood pressure readings (systolic/diastolic)

3 Response confirmation

Patient receives: "✅ Got it. Your nurse has been notified. Keep it up, Maria!"

4 Missed check-in handling

  • If no response by 11 AM, the system flags the patient as "no response today"
  • Clinician reviews the Alerts panel for missed check-ins exceeding 2 days
  • A phone call outreach is logged as an RPM interaction (counts toward CPT minutes)
Patient Tip

Patients can reply STOP at any time to opt out of SMS. If this occurs, notify the care team immediately so the provider can establish an alternative monitoring method before billing is affected.

A3Medication Tracking

Patients receive daily medication reminders. Adherence data feeds into the patient's clinical record and is visible to clinicians.

1 Clinician adds medications

  • In the patient record → Medications tab → Add Medication
  • Enter: drug name, dose, frequency, start date

2 Patient receives daily reminder

SMS example: "MKL Health reminder: Did you take your Lisinopril 10mg today? Reply YES or NO."

3 Adherence recorded

  • YES → marks dose as taken for the day
  • NO → marks as missed; flags for clinician review if 3+ consecutive missed doses
  • Clinician views adherence percentage in the Medications section of the patient chart

A4Viewing Vitals & Trends

Patients do not have a dashboard login. Clinicians review trends on their behalf. This section describes what clinicians see when reviewing patient vitals.

1 Open the patient chart

  • Go to Patients → search by name or MRN
  • Click the patient row to open the full chart

2 Review check-in history

  • The chart shows the last 30 days of check-in data in a trend graph
  • Green = within normal range · Amber = borderline · Red = threshold exceeded

3 Share summary with patient

  • During follow-up calls, verbally share trend data from the chart
  • Example: "Your weight has been stable for 3 weeks — great progress."

A5Alert Escalation & Emergency Guidance

Critical readings trigger an immediate alert. Patients are instructed to call 911 if they are in distress — the platform does not replace emergency services.

⚠ Emergency Protocol

If a patient reports a wellness score of 5, or critical BP readings (>180/110), the system sends an alert AND a clinician must call the patient within 30 minutes. If the patient cannot be reached, follow your practice's emergency escalation protocol and consider contacting EMS.

1 System detects threshold breach

  • Blood pressure systolic > 180 mmHg or diastolic > 110 mmHg
  • Weight gain > 3 lbs in 24 hours or > 5 lbs in 7 days
  • Wellness score of 4 or 5 two days in a row

2 Alert is created and pushed to Slack

The care team channel receives a formatted Slack alert with the patient name, MRN, alert type, recorded value, and threshold. The Alerts panel in the dashboard also shows the alert in red.

3 Clinician calls patient

  • Call the patient's phone directly (not SMS)
  • Assess: is the patient in distress? do they need EMS?
  • Log the call time in the patient chart (this counts as RPM time)

4 Acknowledge alert

  • In the Alerts panel → click the alert → Acknowledge
  • Add a note describing action taken (e.g., "Called patient. BP resolved. Continue monitoring.")
● Clinician

Section B
Clinician Workflows

Clinicians use the MKL Health dashboard daily to monitor patients, respond to alerts, conduct screenings, manage care programs, and ensure all qualifying RPM interactions are captured for billing.

B1Dashboard & Daily Monitoring

The Dashboard is the first screen after login. It provides a real-time snapshot of the patient panel: alerts, check-in compliance, and billing status.

1 Review the morning summary card

  • Open Alerts — number of unacknowledged critical alerts (red badge)
  • Check-ins Today — % of patients who completed morning check-in
  • Patients Near CPT Threshold — patients within 5 minutes of qualifying for 99457/99458

2 Prioritize work order

  • Address red (critical) alerts first
  • Follow up on patients with missed check-ins
  • Log time for patients near billing thresholds

3 End-of-day check

  • Ensure all open alerts are acknowledged with a note
  • Verify any RPM time logged during outreach calls
  • Review TCM episodes with approaching deadlines

B2Patient Management & Records

The Patients section lists all enrolled patients. Clinicians can search, filter, and drill into individual patient charts.

1 Search & filter

  • Use the search bar to find by name, MRN, or phone number
  • Filter by: diagnosis, enrollment type (RPM/CCM/TCM), alert status

2 Open a patient chart

  • Click the patient row → full chart opens
  • Tabs available: Overview, Check-ins, Medications, Alerts, Screenings, Billing, TCM, Notes

3 Log a patient interaction

  • After any call, video visit, or message: click Log Interaction
  • Enter: date, duration in minutes, interaction type (call, SMS review, in-person)
  • Minutes accumulate toward CPT 99457 and 99458 thresholds

4 Add or update clinical notes

  • Use the Notes tab to add free-text clinical observations
  • Notes are timestamped and associated with your provider ID
  • Do not record PHI in Slack messages — use the Notes tab for clinical documentation

B3Alert Response Protocol

Open alerts appear in the Alerts panel and are simultaneously pushed to Slack. All alerts must be acknowledged before end of shift.

1 Navigate to Alerts

Click Alerts in the sidebar. Critical alerts are sorted to the top.

2 Review alert details

  • Patient name, MRN, alert type, recorded value vs. threshold, timestamp
  • Click View Patient to open the full chart in context

3 Take action and acknowledge

  • Take clinical action (call patient, adjust care plan, notify provider)
  • Click Acknowledge → add a mandatory resolution note
  • Alert moves from Open → Resolved; stays visible in chart history

B4Mental Health Screening (PHQ-9 / GAD-7)

The Screening panel administers and scores validated depression and anxiety screenings. Conduct PHQ-9 at enrollment and every 90 days; GAD-7 as indicated.

1 Identify patients due for screening

  • Navigate to Screening
  • The panel shows patients flagged as "Overdue" (no screening in 90+ days)

2 Administer the questionnaire

  • Select the patient → choose PHQ-9 or GAD-7
  • Read each question aloud (or have patient self-complete) and enter responses 0–3
  • Click Calculate Score — the system scores and risk-stratifies automatically

3 Review result and document

  • Score, severity band, and recommended actions appear immediately
  • For PHQ-9 ≥ 10 (moderate): initiate stepped-care protocol per practice guidelines
  • For PHQ-9 question 9 (suicidality) > 0: follow your practice's safety protocol immediately
  • Click Save Result — stored in the patient's screening history
See Appendix II for full PHQ-9 and GAD-7 scoring reference.

B5Care Programs — CCM & TCM

The Care Programs panel manages Chronic Care Management (CCM) and Transitional Care Management (TCM) episodes for qualifying patients.

1 CCM — Chronic Care Management

  • Eligible: patients with 2+ chronic conditions
  • Minimum 20 min/month of care coordination — the system tracks automatically
  • Navigate to Care Programs → CCM tab → select patient → Log CCM Time
  • Enter date, duration, and type of coordination activity

2 TCM — Transitional Care Management

  • Triggered when a patient is discharged from inpatient or observation stay
  • Navigate to Care Programs → TCM → New Episode
  • Enter: hospital discharge date, admission type, diagnosis
  • System calculates: contact-required dates, face-to-face visit deadline

3 TCM milestone deadlines

MilestoneCPT 99495CPT 99496
Interactive contactWithin 14 business daysWithin 7 business days
Face-to-face visitWithin 14 calendar daysWithin 7 calendar days
Medical decision makingModerate complexityHigh complexity
The system displays overdue TCM episodes in red on the Care Programs panel.

B6RPM Billing & CPT Code Capture

MKL Health automates tracking of CMS RPM billing thresholds. Navigate to Billing to view each patient's monthly status.

CPT 99454
~$65/mo
Device supply and data transmission. Automatic when patient completes ≥ 16 days of check-ins in a calendar month.
Requirement ≥ 16 days of device readings in 30-day period. Patient consent on file.
CPT 99457
~$54/mo
First 20 minutes of RPM treatment management. Requires live interactive communication with patient or caregiver.
Requirement ≥ 20 min/month clinical staff time. Must include ≥ 1 interactive contact.
CPT 99458
~$41/unit
Each additional 20-minute increment. Billed in units — up to 2 additional units per month (99458 × 1 and 99458 × 2).
Requirement Each additional 20 min of clinical time after first 99457.

1 View billing dashboard

  • Navigate to Billing → view current month's summary
  • Each patient row shows: days enrolled, minutes logged, CPT codes met (✓ or pending)

2 Log RPM interaction time

  • Open a patient chart → Log RPM Time
  • Enter: date, minutes, type (data review, care coordination, patient communication)
  • System updates CPT qualification status in real time

3 Export billing data

  • At month end: Billing → Export CSV
  • CSV contains all qualifying encounters, CPT codes, patient identifiers, and provider NPI
  • Submit to your billing team or EHR for claim submission

B7Calendar & Appointment Management

The Calendar panel syncs with Google Calendar to surface upcoming patient appointments alongside RPM monitoring data.

1 Connect Google Calendar

  • First-time setup: navigate to Calendar → click Connect Google Calendar
  • Authorize with your work Google account

2 View upcoming appointments

  • Calendar shows the next 30 days of events from your connected calendar
  • Patient appointments appear alongside internal care team meetings

3 Create a follow-up appointment

  • In a patient chart → Schedule Follow-up
  • Select date/time → the event is created in Google Calendar and linked to the patient record

B8Team Messaging via Slack

The Slack panel lets you view the care team channel and send messages without leaving the dashboard. Clinical alerts are automatically posted here.

⚠ HIPAA Notice

Do not post patient names, MRNs, dates of birth, or clinical details in Slack messages. Use patient initials + MRN last 4 digits only. All detailed clinical notes belong in the patient's chart Notes tab.

1 View care team messages

  • Navigate to Slack → the panel shows the last 20 messages in the configured channel
  • Auto-generated alerts appear with 🚨 header and red formatting

2 Send a team message

  • Type in the message field → press Send
  • Messages appear in the Slack channel immediately

Section C
Admin Workflows

Admins configure and maintain the MKL Health environment. All admin functions are accessed through the Setup panel (sidebar → Setup) and the Vercel dashboard for infrastructure-level changes.

C1User Provisioning & Access Control

User accounts are managed through Supabase Authentication. Admins create, deactivate, and assign roles directly in the Supabase dashboard.

1 Create a new clinician account

  • Log in to supabase.com → select the MKL Health project
  • Go to Authentication → Users → Invite User
  • Enter the clinician's work email address → click Send Invite
  • The clinician receives an email with a magic link to set their password

2 Assign a role

  • In Supabase → Authentication → Users → find the new user
  • Edit the user metadata: set role field to clinician or admin
  • The app reads this role at login to control access to Setup and admin-only features

3 Deactivate a departing user

  • In Supabase → Authentication → Users → find the user
  • Click Ban User to immediately revoke access
  • Rotate any shared API keys the departing user had access to
⚠ HIPAA Requirement: Deactivate accounts on the last day of employment, not retroactively.

C3Airtable & Backlog Management

The Backlog panel connects to Airtable to display development stories and sprint status. Admins and product managers use this for internal project tracking.

1 Create a new story

  • Navigate to Backlog → click New Story
  • Enter: title, description, story points, type (feature/bug/chore)
  • Story is created in the linked Airtable base immediately

2 Manage sprints

  • Click New Sprint to create a sprint with a start/end date
  • Drag stories into the sprint from the backlog
  • The active sprint displays on the Backlog panel's Sprint view

3 Update Airtable base configuration

  • To change which Airtable base the backlog reads from, update AIRTABLE_BASE_ID in Vercel
  • Table names must match the expected schema: "Stories" and "Sprints" tables with standard field names

C4Slack Channel & Alert Configuration

All clinical alerts route to the Slack channel specified by SLACK_CHANNEL_ID. Admins configure which alerts fire and where they route.

1 Change the alert destination channel

  • In Slack: right-click the target channel → View channel details → copy the Channel ID from the bottom of the About section
  • In Vercel: update SLACK_CHANNEL_ID → redeploy

2 Verify the Slack bot has channel access

  • In Slack: go to the target channel → Integrations → confirm the MKL Health bot is a member
  • If not: type /invite @MKL Health Bot in the channel

3 Test alert delivery

  • In the app Setup panel: click Send Test Alert
  • Confirm the formatted alert appears in the Slack channel within 30 seconds

C5Billing Oversight & CSV Export

Admins review the monthly billing summary across all providers and export data for the billing department or EHR submission.

1 Review monthly billing dashboard

  • Navigate to Billing → select month from the dropdown
  • Top cards: total qualifying patients per CPT code, projected revenue
  • Table: per-patient breakdown (provider, CPT codes met, days enrolled, minutes logged)

2 Export billing CSV

  • Click Export CSV — downloads a file named rpm-billing-YYYY-MM.csv
  • Columns: Patient ID, Patient Name, Provider NPI, CPT Codes, Units, Days Enrolled, Minutes Logged, Period
  • Send to your billing team by the 3rd of the following month

3 Investigate discrepancies

  • If a patient shows fewer days than expected: check for missed check-ins in the patient chart
  • If minutes are low: remind providers to log all RPM interaction time including data review and coordination calls

C6Compliance, Audit Logs & HIPAA

MKL Health is designed for HIPAA-adjacent operation. The following controls are in place; Admins are responsible for enforcing operational compliance.

ControlHow It's ImplementedAdmin Action
Access ControlRole-based Supabase auth; JWT on every API callProvision and deprovision users promptly
Encryption in transitHTTPS enforced by Vercel on all routesVerify SSL cert is valid; Vercel auto-renews
Encryption at restSupabase PostgreSQL with AES-256No action required; confirm in Supabase settings
Audit loggingSupabase logs all DB read/write with user ID + timestampExport logs quarterly; retain 6 years minimum
PHI minimization in SlackPolicy; not technically enforcedTrain staff; review Slack messages weekly for PHI violations
BAA coverageSupabase offers a BAA (paid plan); Vercel BAA available on EnterpriseEnsure BAAs are signed before going live with patient data

1 Quarterly audit log review

  • Log in to Supabase → Logs → API logs
  • Filter by date range → export to CSV
  • Review for: unauthorized access patterns, unusual data volumes, off-hours access
  • Retain logs per your practice's retention policy (minimum 6 years under HIPAA)

2 Security incident response

  • Suspected breach: immediately ban affected user accounts in Supabase
  • Rotate all API keys in Vercel environment variables
  • Notify your HIPAA Privacy Officer within 24 hours
  • Document the incident: timeline, affected data, remediation steps
HIPAA Breach Notification Rule: notify affected patients within 60 days; notify HHS if > 500 individuals affected.
